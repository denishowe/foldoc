2015-01-03 - Ray Saunders: In 1965, IBM's SDD refused to admit the existence of Supserzap. When I held up an executable deck labeled SUPERZAP in big red letters, they looked at me and said: "Superzap Does Not Exist"., accompanied by severe expressions and implied threats of terminating my IBM career. I understood, but I had work to do. I tracked down the programmer who wrote it. He gladly gave me a deck and his own documentation. I smuggled the code out to various IBM Field Systems Center folks. I knew that once it got out, SDD would have to admit it and make it official - it's too useful to deny to folks in the field. The FE division was responsible for fixes - at least in theory - and I figured it would make their life much easier. It did, and within a year or so, it became an official Utility program. It can zap any disk data, not just code. It's the only utility I know of that can fix a broken VTOC, for example. And the name has nothing to do with the Zero-and-Add-Packed instruction. The meaning of 'zap' in this context predates computers - think all those SciFi movies where the hero zaps the aliens. Been There. Done That.
